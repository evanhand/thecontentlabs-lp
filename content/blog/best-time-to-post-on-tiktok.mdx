Every "best time to post on TikTok" article says the same thing.

*"Post at 6pm. That's when everyone's scrolling."*

We checked. It's wrong.

We pulled the exact posted timestamps of **3,000+ videos** from creators on TikTok and Instagram Reels, then correlated them with actual performance. The data tells a completely different story from the conventional wisdom.

Here's what we found.

## The short answer

**The best time to post is 9am to 11am Eastern Time.**

Not evening. Not late night. Morning. Specifically, 9am ET has the highest average views in our entire dataset.

## Why 9am beats 6pm

The conventional wisdom is that everyone is scrolling in the evening after work. That part is true. The part everyone gets wrong is what the algorithm does with it.

When you post at 6pm:
- You're fighting every other creator who followed the same advice
- Algorithm distribution is saturated with similar content
- Your video competes for attention during peak supply, not peak demand

When you post at 9am:
- Far fewer creators are posting
- Algorithm has more room to push your content out
- Morning scrollers (commute, breakfast, procrastinating at work) have fresh feeds with less competition
- Your video gets distribution throughout the entire day as more people come online

<Callout type="tip">
The "best time to post" isn't when the most people are watching. It's when the algorithm can actually push your content to the most people. Those aren't the same thing.
</Callout>

---

## The early morning graveyard

Don't post between 4am and 7am ET. The data is brutal:

- **5am**: 2,282 avg views
- **6am**: 6,756 avg views
- **7am**: 39,455 avg views

There's nobody awake. The algorithm has no early signal to push your content, so it effectively stalls. By the time people start scrolling at 8-9am, your video is already buried under everyone else's fresh uploads.

This is why that "3am engagement hack" you've seen on TikTok tips accounts is fake. The data doesn't back it up.

## One thing to remember

Timing is a multiplier, not the whole equation. A mediocre video posted at 9am on Sunday will still underperform a great video posted at 3pm on Wednesday.

But when you have a strong hook, good content, and a clean edit, **posting time can be the difference between 50K views and 300K views.**

Every other variable you've optimized (hooks, pacing, format) compounds with timing. Get the timing right and you're giving your best content the best possible distribution.

Post at 9am. Post on Sunday when you can. And stop listening to the "post at 6pm" crowd.
