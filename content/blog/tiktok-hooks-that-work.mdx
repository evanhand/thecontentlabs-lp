<HookSection rank={1} name="Hot Take" tagline="Say something bold. Force people to agree or disagree." engagement="7.8%" videos="1,058" topViews="1.9M">

The simplest hook there is. You open your video with a strong, opinionated statement. No setup. No context. Just a take that makes people feel something.

People can't scroll past something they disagree with. They have to watch to see if you back it up. And if they agree, they share it to prove a point.

**Real examples from the data:**

<HookExample quote="The Bengals should be ASHAMED of how they handled Joe Burrow" creator="benlikessports" views="1.8M" url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7550011627542760735" />
<HookExample quote="We just witnessed the single greatest game in MLB history" creator="benlikessports" views="1.7M" url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7562391402626256159" />
<HookExample quote="If you look up the word 'dog' in the dictionary, the only thing that pops up is Baker Mayfield" creator="benlikessports" views="195K" url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7550502340256697630" />

**Watch this one in action:**

<TikTokEmbed url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7550011627542760735" caption="'The Bengals should be ASHAMED' - @benlikessports, 1.8M views" />

This hook type has the **highest average engagement** of anything in the dataset. It's built for comments. People argue, tag friends, and quote it in their own videos.

<WhenToUse>When you have a strong opinion about something your audience cares about. Sports, pop culture, industry drama, anything with a built-in fanbase that loves to debate.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={2} name="Flip the Script" tagline="Say the opposite of what people expect. Challenge what everyone assumes is true." engagement="6.3%" videos="1,306" topViews="17.7M">

You open by contradicting conventional wisdom. You tell people the thing they believe is wrong. Their brain goes "wait, what?" and now they're watching.

Different from a Hot Take because you're not just sharing an opinion. You're specifically going against what most people think.

**Real examples from the data:**

<HookExample quote="STOP using ChatGPT. It's outdated and falling behind." creator="evhandd" views="102K" />
<HookExample quote="If my kids ask who the greatest tight end of all time is, I'm telling them number 87... from the New England Patriots" creator="benlikessports" views="164K" url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7541174193799384350" />

**Watch this one in action:**

<TikTokEmbed url="https://www.tiktok.com/@benlikessports/video/7541174193799384350" caption="'Greatest tight end... from the Patriots' - @benlikessports, 164K views" />

This hook type has the **highest ceiling** in the dataset. The top Flip the Script video hit 17.7M views. When you nail one, it goes massive because people share it to start arguments.

<WhenToUse>When you can challenge a popular belief and back it up. Works for educational content, myth-busting, and anything where the audience has a strong default assumption you can flip.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={3} name="Talk to the Camera" tagline="No intro, no graphics. Just talk like you're FaceTiming a friend." engagement="6.1%" videos="448" topViews="6.8M">

You pick up the phone and start talking. Mid-thought. No setup. No "hey guys, welcome back." Just raw, unfiltered energy like you're in a conversation.

In a feed full of polished, over-produced content, this stands out because it feels personal. Like the creator is talking directly to you, not performing for an audience.

**Real examples from the data:**

<HookExample quote="Let me tell you something. When you are brave enough..." creator="higherupwellness" views="131K" />
<HookExample quote="Does anyone care to fill me in on what's happening?" creator="higherupwellness" views="162K" />

**Watch this one in action:**

<InstagramEmbed url="https://www.instagram.com/p/DBzVkxqS7sy" caption="'This New York Boston rivalry goes so deep' - @kentsports24, 5.7M views" />

Fewer views on average than other types, but the engagement rate is high because people who watch feel a real connection. They comment. They reply. They share it saying "this is literally me."

<WhenToUse>When you're speaking from personal experience, frustration, or genuine emotion. Rants, motivational content, reactions, anything where being real matters more than being polished.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={4} name="Pull Them In" tagline="Open with a question, a mystery, or a 'wait... what?' moment that creates an open loop." engagement="6.1%" videos="2,041" topViews="69.7M">

You ask a question or set up a mystery in the first few seconds. The viewer's brain needs an answer, and the only way to get it is to keep watching.

This is the **most common hook type** in the entire dataset (2,041 videos) and it holds the record for the single highest-viewed video at **69.7 million views**.

If you make educational content, explainers, or breakdown videos, this is your bread and butter. It also pulls the **highest average views** of any hook type at 501K per video.

<WhenToUse>When you're breaking down a story, explaining something complex, or investigating a topic. Works across every niche. If there's a "why" or "how" your audience wants answered, lead with the question.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={5} name="Show Something Wild" tagline="Open with something the viewer has never seen before. Let the visual do the talking." engagement="5.4%" videos="524" topViews="24.2M">

Instead of telling people something is impressive, you show them. The first frame is something unexpected, futuristic, or "how is this possible?" and that visual alone stops the scroll.

You're not making a claim. You're showing proof. The hook IS the demo.

**Real examples from the data:**

<HookExample quote="AI voices with emotion are finally here" creator="benkaluza" views="52K" />

This hook type pulls the **second-highest average views** at 514K. It's the go-to for tech demos, product reveals, cooking transformations, before/afters, and anything where seeing is believing.

<WhenToUse>When you have something visually surprising to show. Tech demos, product reveals, transformations, anything where the first few seconds are visually "whoa." Don't describe it. Show it.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={6} name="Predict the Future" tagline="Tell people what's about to happen. Make a prediction they can't ignore." engagement="5.4%" videos="774" topViews="14.4M">

You open by telling the viewer what's coming next. You're positioning yourself as someone who sees around corners, and people watch to find out if you're right.

Predictions are shareable by nature. People either want to prove you wrong later, or bookmark you so they can say "I told you so."

<WhenToUse>When you have a strong read on where something is headed. Works well in tech, sports, finance, and any niche where there's always a "what happens next?" angle.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={7} name="Lead with Proof" tagline="Open with a specific number, result, or piece of evidence. Let the data do the talking." engagement="5.2%" videos="461" topViews="28.1M">

You skip the intro and drop a number in the first second. A stat, a result, a before/after. Something specific that registers as credible and makes people want the full story.

Numbers are pattern interrupts. Your brain processes "50,000 followers in 4 posts" differently than "I grew my account fast." Specificity stops the scroll.

<WhenToUse>When you have real numbers, real results, or a stat that makes people stop. Case studies, listicles, tool reviews, and anything where you can lead with evidence instead of opinion.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

## How to pick the right hook

This isn't about memorizing 7 categories. It's about matching the hook to the content you're making.

<NumberBlock items={[
  { number: "01", text: "Strong opinion about something? Use a Hot Take or Flip the Script." },
  { number: "02", text: "Telling a story or reacting to something? Talk to the Camera." },
  { number: "03", text: "Explaining or breaking something down? Pull Them In with a question." },
  { number: "04", text: "Showing something visual or new? Show Something Wild." },
  { number: "05", text: "Making a prediction or calling a trend? Predict the Future." },
  { number: "06", text: "Have numbers or results to share? Lead with Proof." }
]} />

The best creators rotate between hook types. One Flip the Script, one Pull Them In breakdown, one Lead with Proof case study. The variety keeps both the algorithm and the audience engaged.
