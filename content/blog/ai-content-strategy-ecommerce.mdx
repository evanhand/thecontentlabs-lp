E-commerce is the **highest-variance niche** in our dataset. The average ecom video pulls **277,862 views** — but the median is only 5,121. That gap means a few videos are winning massively and most are stuck.

The difference between brands averaging 2M views and brands averaging 11K isn't budget. It's pattern choice.

The most extreme gap in any niche we've analyzed. **Magician hooks** — unexpected visual reveals, product doing something the viewer didn't anticipate — averaged **2.1M views** in ecom. **Teacher hooks** (the product explainer default) averaged **11,543**.

That's 186× worse for Teacher than Magician. Same product, wildly different outcomes.

Example Magician hooks for ecom:
- Product pops open / transforms / reveals something mid-video
- Unexpected use case ("watch what this actually does with...")
- Silent-first-frame + visual payoff structure

DTC brands default to Aspiration — polished lifestyle content that says *"imagine this product in your life."* It averages 26,697 views in our data.

**Humor** averaged 262,568. **Curiosity** averaged 488,556.

The lesson: reach is earned by provocation, not polish. A lower-production Humor video where someone just roasts the product's existence outperforms a beautifully-shot Aspiration spot 10×.

This doesn't mean skip Desire — Desire is still your conversion driver. It means layer Desire into the *middle* of a video that opens with Humor or Curiosity. Reach then conversion. Not the other way.

## Why 1-in-12 ecom videos gets stuck

The 8.6% stuck rate in ecom is real. Looking at the stuck videos, three patterns dominate:

1. **Teacher hook + Aspiration trigger + sub-20s length.** The "clean DTC ad" pattern. It rarely breaks.
2. **Product-first hook with no human interaction** — opening with the product on a white background is a categorization disaster. Algorithm can't tell what niche it's in.
3. **Over-produced content with no obvious reason to watch past 3 seconds.** Polish ≠ hook.

Any one of those being fixed breaks most of the stuck pattern.

## Length: 30-90 seconds, content-dependent

Ecom median in our data was 54.5 seconds. But the highest-performers (Magician + Curiosity) often went 30-45 seconds to land the reveal fast. Humor content ran longer — 60-90s — because the joke needed a build.

Let the hook archetype dictate length, not the other way around.

## The bottom line

E-commerce has a bigger upside than almost any other niche — a Magician + Curiosity hit in ecom can pull 2M+ views. But the default DTC content pattern (Teacher + Aspiration + short polished spot) is one of the worst performers in our entire dataset, averaging 11K-26K views.

Swap to Magician or Humor hooks. Drop Aspiration as your primary emotion. Let Desire layer in for conversion, but earn reach with Curiosity first.
