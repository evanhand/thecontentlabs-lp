## What's actually shifting

<Callout type="tip">
  <strong>↑ Predictions are the new currency.</strong> Up 8% vs prior 90 days. AI volatility, sports drama, contract chaos, breaking news. People are anxious about what's coming next, and "I can see around the corner" wins their attention. Specificity (numbers, deadlines, named events) is what flips a generic prediction into a credible one.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  <strong>↓ Hot Takes are saturated.</strong> Engagement crashed from 6.60% to 4.70% in 90 days. The algorithm still pushes them (highest avg views by far), but viewers are scrolling past more often. Everyone running the "you NEED to hear this" playbook is fighting for the same shrinking pie.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  <strong>↓ Personal-story openers are collapsing.</strong> Down 40% in 90 days. Storytelling isn't dead, the *opening pattern* is. When viewers can predict the next 10 seconds from the first 2, they swipe. Lead with the surprise or the consequence, not "this is me when..."
</Callout>

## Real videos pulling engagement right now

Verbatim hooks from the last 30 days.

<HookSection rank={1} name="Predict the Future" tagline="Tell people what's about to happen. Position yourself as the person who saw it first." engagement="5.93%" videos="68" topViews="328K">

**Status:** Climbing. **+0.44pt** vs prior 90 days. The only hook type rising in May 2026.

**Pattern:** Open with a prediction, a number, or a "watch what happens next" frame. Anchor it to a specific date, dollar amount, or breaking event. The viewer's brain treats it like a movie trailer.

<HookExample quote="The Masters will be making $277 every single second starting this Sunday until the Masters is over." creator="wunderpar" views="328K" />
<HookExample quote="The US government is about to completely blow up the NFL's TV contracts. And if they win, you may never have to pay for another streaming service just to watch a game ever again." creator="evhandagain" views="250.9K" />
<HookExample quote="We have some insane breaking news in the golf world right now. If the news out of New York is true, this is going to change absolutely everything for the game of golf." creator="wunderpar" views="257.8K" />

<WhenToUse>When you have an angle on what's coming next in your niche. Industry shifts, breaking news, scheduled events, contract deadlines, AI releases. Specificity makes it credible.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={2} name="Flip the Script" tagline="Say the opposite of what everyone assumes. Make people argue in the comments." engagement="5.48%" videos="153" topViews="2.1M">

**Status:** Flat. **-0.19pt** vs prior 90 days. The most reliable hook type across our entire dataset.

**Pattern:** "Everyone thinks X, but the reality is Y." The single highest-viewed video in the last 30 days (2.1M) was a Flip the Script hook. People share videos that prove their existing opinion right or refute the dominant narrative.

<HookExample quote="Everyone thinks Diego Pavia nuked his career after the Heisman ceremony with this stunt that he pulled, but the reality is NFL GMs are looking for any reason to pass on this dude anyway." creator="evhandagain" views="2.1M" />
<HookExample quote="I don't get the sense I'll be the fan favorite but..." creator="wunderpargolf" views="791K" />
<HookExample quote="Unpopular opinion. And one of the creators who is on here constantly complaining about the TikTok algorithm." creator="nicoledanilamoureux" views="1.9K (27% engagement)" />

<WhenToUse>When you can credibly contradict the dominant take. Sports, politics, marketing, finance. Any niche with a popular narrative you can flip with evidence.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={3} name="Pull Them In" tagline="Open a mystery. Set up a question that the viewer has to watch to resolve." engagement="4.71%" videos="164" topViews="1.5M">

**Status:** Cooling. **-1.28pt** vs prior 90 days. Still drives huge views (1.5M top video), but the average is fatiguing.

**Pattern:** Open-loop question or "wait, what?" moment. Generic "here's what nobody told you about X" hooks are getting filtered. The mystery itself has to be specific and unfamiliar to break through now.

<HookExample quote="This guy just created the world's biggest headache of Dan Campbell's coaching career so far." creator="evhandagain" views="432K" />
<HookExample quote="Here we are. The most confusing place on the golf course." creator="wunderpargolf" views="1.5M" />
<HookExample quote="What do you suppose is going on with my eyeball right now? It hurts like an MF'er." creator="nicoledanilamoureux" views="1.3K (13.55% engagement)" />

<WhenToUse>When you have a real mystery, breakdown, or behind-the-scenes angle. Investigations, explainers, "wait, what happened" stories. Skip generic open-loops.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={4} name="Hot Take" tagline="Lead with a strong opinion. Force the audience to agree or disagree." engagement="4.70%" videos="220" topViews="1.9M">

**Status:** Cooling fast. **-1.90pt** vs prior 90 days, the biggest decline in the dataset. Still drives the highest avg views (129K), but per-view engagement is the issue.

**Pattern:** The 1.9M-view Rory McIlroy hook worked because the take was specific, timely, and grounded in a real moment. Generic "controversial opinion" hooks are getting scrolled past.

<HookExample quote="Rory McIlroy just reminded all of us how poor we really are." creator="evhandagain" views="1.9M" />
<HookExample quote="This guy is already having the worst Master Sunday in the history of golf." creator="wunderpar" views="1.1M" />
<HookExample quote="This is gonna piss a lot of people off." creator="nicoledanilamoureux" views="1.7K (17.82% engagement)" />

<WhenToUse>When you have a take you can actually back up. Specific, timely, opinionated. Skip if you're just trying to be edgy without a point.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={5} name="Drop a Useful Fact" tagline="Lead with a piece of useful information. 'Did you know' or 'turn off this setting' formats." engagement="4.56%" videos="119" topViews="526K">

**Status:** Flat. **-0.07pt** vs prior 90 days. Steady because it actually delivers value, not just emotion.

**Pattern:** Lower view counts on average, higher engagement-per-view. Saves and shares run higher than other hook types. The audience that watches is invested.

<HookExample quote="Did you know that Boise State has a legal monopoly on college football fields?" creator="evhandagain" views="37K" />
<HookExample quote="Turn off this setting in TikTok immediately." creator="nicoledanilamoureux" views="89.3K" />
<HookExample quote="I'm going to tell you exactly why TikTok Shop wants you to niche down. As a CEO of a seven-figure organization who spends money on advertising." creator="nicoledanilamoureux" views="1.9K (17.88% engagement)" />

<WhenToUse>When you have specific, useful information your audience can apply immediately. Settings, hacks, "did you know" facts, tactical advice with a number attached.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>

<HookSection rank={6} name="Tell a Story" tagline="Open with a personal narrative. 'This is me when...' or 'Let me tell you about...' formats." engagement="4.14%" videos="25" topViews="73K">

**Status:** Collapsing. **-2.79pt** vs prior 90 days. A 40% engagement crash.

**Pattern:** The story format isn't dead, the *opening* is. Viewers can predict the structure from the first 2 seconds and they swipe. Lead with the surprise or the consequence, not the setup.

<HookExample quote="this is me standing up for myself in a job interview" creator="realisticrecruiting" views="72.7K" />
<HookExample quote="I got laid off at the height of my career and it crushed me mentally in a way that I am not proud of." creator="RealisticRecruiting" views="6.2K" />

<WhenToUse>Only when the story itself is unusual or the stakes are unmistakable in the first 2 seconds. Skip the "this is me when" opener entirely.</WhenToUse>

</HookSection>
