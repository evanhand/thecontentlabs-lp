## What's actually happening

### Predictions are the new currency

Predict-the-Future hooks (the "here's what's about to happen" or "watch what they're going to do" format) are the only hook type climbing. Engagement is up 8% vs the prior 90 days, even as overall feed engagement has flattened.

Why now? AI volatility, sports drama, breaking news cycles, and contract chaos. People are anxious about what's coming next, and the hook that says "I can see around the corner" wins their attention.

The format has built-in shareability. Viewers either bookmark you to verify later or share it to start a conversation. Both extend the video's reach past the initial view.

### Hot Takes are saturated

The biggest story in the data is the collapse of Hot Take engagement. From 6.60% down to 4.70% in 90 days. They still drive the most views per video by a wide margin (avg 129,912), but the per-view engagement is fatiguing.

Translation: the algorithm is still pushing them, but viewers are scrolling past more often. Everyone running the "you NEED to hear this" playbook is fighting for the same shrinking pie.

You can still win with Hot Takes in May 2026. You just have to be sharper, more specific, and have an actual take, not just a tone.

### Personal-story hooks are collapsing

Tell-a-Story hooks dropped 2.79 points. That's a 40% engagement crash in 90 days. The "let me tell you about the time I..." opener that worked through Q1 is now being skipped.

This isn't because storytelling died. It's because the *opening pattern* got memed. When viewers can predict the next 10 seconds from the first 2, they swipe.
