The top-performing hook in coaching is **Hot Take.** Strong opinion, stated directly, upfront. Averaged 198,615 views across 17 videos. **4× the niche average.**

Teacher hooks (the default "let me coach you through this" pattern) averaged 46,042. Less than a quarter of what Hot Takes pull.

Example Hot Take hooks that win in coaching:

- *"Stop giving your clients homework. It doesn't work."*
- *"The 'riches are in the niches' advice killed my business for 2 years."*
- *"Coaching certifications are mostly a scam and here's why."*

Each takes a strong position on something coaches typically believe. Friction creates reach.

The disaster in coaching content: Trust-based videos averaged **2,457 views.** 0.03× the niche median.

Curiosity averaged 94K. A **38× gap** between the two emotions coaches default to.

Why does Trust underperform so badly? Coaching creators use it as a proxy for "establishing credibility before getting to the goods." The algorithm doesn't read credentials. It reads retention, engagement velocity, and categorization speed. Trust-building content gets buried because it doesn't provoke fast enough.

Lead with the provocative idea. Let Trust accumulate across many videos, not within a single one.

## Why coaching gets stuck more than finance

Finance creators default to Investigator plus Curiosity (the winning combo in their niche). Coaching creators default to Teacher plus Trust (the losing combo in theirs). That one pattern difference explains most of the gap between a 1.2% stuck rate and a 10.1% stuck rate.

## Length: 60 to 90 seconds

Coaching median was 63 seconds. Stretch toward the longer end. Coaching content needs:

- The setup (3 to 5s)
- The reframe / claim (10 to 15s)
- The reasoning / evidence (20 to 40s)
- The takeaway (10 to 15s)

Rush that into 15 to 25 seconds and the video feels shallow. 60 to 90 seconds lets the reframe land with enough weight to get shared.
